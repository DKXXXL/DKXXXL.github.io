Question

Consider a Mobius Band MMM and the closed unit disk DDD in R2\reals^2R2

Glue the boundary of DDD to the boundary of MMM.

The boundary of DDD should go around the boundary of M exactly once. Prove that the space you obtain is the projective plane RP2\reals \mathbb{P}^2RP2.

Give a hand waving proof of this fact, this means, make drawings and brief comments that are convincing and careful but you do not have to provide the explicit quotient maps.

Solution:

Given arbitrary mobius band MMM.

We have already known, according to the lecture note, and reasoning in Q3, after we cut mobius band in half, we get the following square with "gluing annotation" stands for mobius.

We can see there is only one boundary, with two kinds of orientation -- AB(D)CAB(D)CAB(D)C and A(C)D(B)AA(C)D(B)AA(C)D(B)A.

Now given the disc DDD, with two points annotated on it, we also have two orientation -- EFGHEFGHEFGH or EHGFEHGFEHGF.

Since the two boundary will need to glue to each other "exactly once", that means we will stick to one orientation through the gluing -- We need to glue EFGHEFGHEFGH to AB(D)CAB(D)CAB(D)C or to A(C)D(B)AA(C)D(B)AA(C)D(B)A, this is the only two possibilities.

To make things clear, let's cut the disc into two and first glue each semicircle onto the square and then glue the semicirlces back. In other words, we would have the following two semicircles:

We have two ways to glue:

(Note, in the following, all the sequence of points has order information, i.e. ABABAB is just AB⃗\vec{AB}AB)

That means we will glue E1FG1E_1FG_1E1​FG1​ to ABABAB and G2HE2G_2HE_2G2​HE2​ to DCDCDC, and then we will need to glue E1G1E_1G_1E1​G1​ to E2G2E_2G_2E2​G2​ as well.

And we will have the following picture:

The two different green arrow explains how E1FG1E_1FG_1E1​FG1​ to ABABAB and G2HE2G_2HE_2G2​HE2​ to DCDCDC. As the next step, we will need to glue E1G1E_1G_1E1​G1​ back to E2G2E_2G_2E2​G2​, where the blue arrows indicate the gluing strategy.

After the two semi-circles are glued, we can see that this is whole picture is exactly the definition of Projective Space, module rotations.

glue EFGHEFGHEFGH to A(C)D(B)AA(C)D(B)AA(C)D(B)A

This means we need to glue E1FG1E_1FG_1E1​FG1​ to CDCDCD; G2HE2G_2HE_2G2​HE2​ to BABABA. and finally E2G2E_2G_2E2​G2​ to E1G1E_1G_1E1​G1​ (in blue arrow)

This turns out to be a projective space as well.
